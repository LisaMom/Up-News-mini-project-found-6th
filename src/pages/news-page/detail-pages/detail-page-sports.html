Lionel Messi's Sublime First Touch Leads To Historic World Cup Goal

Argentina survived a tense knockout night after Messi turned one difficult pass into the moment that changed the match.

All News/Sports/Football

1

Historic Goal

AET

Extra Time

R16

World Cup Stage

Lionel Messi produced another defining World Cup moment with a first touch that turned pressure into control and gave Argentina the breakthrough it desperately needed.

The match had been uncomfortable from the start. Cape Verde defended with courage, broke quickly into open space, and forced Argentina into long spells of sideways possession that brought more tension than threat.

Messi changed the rhythm after halftime. Dropping between midfield and attack, he began receiving under pressure and pulling defenders toward him, creating the gaps Argentina had failed to find earlier.

The goal arrived from a pass that looked slightly behind him. Messi cushioned it with one touch, shifted his body away from the nearest defender, and finished before the goalkeeper could reset.

Argentina still had to survive a late scare and extra-time pressure, but the goal gave the team the composure it had been missing. By the final whistle, the match felt less like a routine win and more like another chapter in Messi's long tournament mythology.

Some players need space to make the game slow down. Messi creates that space with the first touch itself.
