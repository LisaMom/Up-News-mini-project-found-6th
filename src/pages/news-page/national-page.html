New Thai razor wire barricades spark fresh diplomatic protests

PM orders stronger enforcement of fisheries crimes, calls for instigators to be prosecuted

Prime Minister Hun Manet has directed the armed forces and ministries to intensify action against illegal fishing and prosecute organisers. At National Fish Day in Svay Rieng, he oversaw the release of 1.5 million juvenile fish into the Vaico River, stressing that enforcement has helped fish stocks recover. Manet urged stronger control of drag‑net operations and electric fishing, saying offenders must be stopped to protect livelihoods and national resources.

Cambodia confirms readiness to host historic Francophonie Summit — Paris meeting sets stage for November

Cambodia has reaffirmed its readiness to host the 20th Francophonie Summit in Phnom Penh on November 15–16, with Foreign Minister Prak Sokhonn assuring member states that preparations are on track. The summit, themed “Reinvesting in Peace for Shared Prosperity and Sustainable Stability,” will mark a historic diplomatic milestone, making Cambodia the first Southeast Asian nation in nearly three decades to host the event. The Paris ministerial meeting also advanced the selection of the next OIF Secretary‑General, with final decisions expected at the Phnom Penh summit.

Jail overcrowding reaching record highs, warns prison director

Cambodia’s prisons added 5,000 detainees in the first half of 2026, driven by crackdowns on scams, gangs, drugs, and crime. Projections show numbers could reach 85,000 by 2028, raising serious concerns over overcrowding and staff shortages. Officials warn cramped conditions — 1 m² per detainee versus the UN’s 3 m² standard — risk riots and escapes. The Interior Ministry is considering more staff, expanded facilities, and non‑custodial measures like parole and community service to ease pressure.

Cambodia promoting legal ‘trust framework’ to boost foreign investment confidence

Cambodia is promoting its legal trust system to boost foreign investment and protect assets. At EuroCham’s July 2 forum, regulators stressed that registered trusts give investors legal certainty, while experts urged clearer tax rules to encourage wider adoption.

Cambodian SMEs offered loans of up to $1 million for energy‑saving upgrades

Cambodian SMEs can now access loans up to $1 million at 5.5% interest for energy‑efficient equipment and renewable technology. The new Energy Efficiency Financing Scheme, backed by a $20 million revolving fund, aims to cut costs, boost competitiveness, and support the Kingdom’s clean‑energy goals.

Local vehicle assembly industry takes major step as Toyota begins local assembly of Hilux Travo

Toyota Tsusho Manufacturing Cambodia has inaugurated a new Hilux Travo pickup assembly line at the Phnom Penh SEZ (July 2). Deputy PM Sun Chanthol hailed the project as proof of Cambodia’s industrial growth, creating jobs, boosting skills, and strengthening the domestic supply chain. Toyota’s investment reflects strong confidence in Cambodia’s economic potential and showcases the capability of local engineers and workers.

Jan–June Angkor ticket sales plummet by almost a third in first half of year

Cambodia’s Angkor Archaeological Park saw foreign visitors fall to 387,772 in Jan–Jun 2026, a 31.7% decline from last year. Revenue dropped to $18.47 million, down nearly 30%, amid global uncertainties and Thai border tensions. Other sites like Koh Ker and Beng Mealea also reported modest earnings, highlighting ongoing struggles in Cambodia’s tourism recovery.
